<h1>National Pre-hospital Emergency Care Workshop</h1>
<h1></h1>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Amb_Team_National_PEC_workshop.jpeg">
</div>
<p>ITE has concluded the National Pre-hospital Emergency Care Workshop on
25 June 2024. In previous sessions, ITE has covered patient assessment,
as well as common medical and trauma conditions. The session on 25 June
2024 focused on revisiting trauma conditions, aiming to refresh and enhance
both theoretical foundations and practical skills.</p>
<h3>Lecture Summaries:</h3>
<p><strong>Lecture 1: Bleeding and Shock</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Content:</strong> Pathophysiology and recognition of the signs
and symptoms of hemorrhagic shock.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Focus:</strong> Management of hemorrhagic shock in the pre-hospital
setting using the Patient Assessment Model (PAM).</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Lecture 2: Penetrating Injury</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Content:</strong> Specific areas of the body affected by penetrating
injuries.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Focus:</strong> Emphasizing safe practices, preventing further
complications, and managing casualties with stab wounds in the pre-hospital
setting using PAM.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Lecture 3: Head Injury</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Content:</strong> Various types of head injuries, from skull fractures
to Traumatic Brain Injuries (TBI).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Focus:</strong> Recognition of signs and symptoms of head injuries
and their management in the pre-hospital setting using PAM.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3>Skills Stations:</h3>
<p><strong>Skills Station 1: Management of Patient with Hemorrhagic Shock</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Objective:</strong> Participants will recognize the signs and symptoms
of shock and manage a simulated patient in hemorrhagic shock in the pre-hospital
setting.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Skills Station 2: Management of Patient with Head Injury</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Objective:</strong> Participants will recognize the signs and symptoms
of head injury and manage a simulated patient with head injury in the pre-hospital
setting.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The next workshop is scheduled for December 2024. Please keep an eye out
for it and register closer to the date.</p>
